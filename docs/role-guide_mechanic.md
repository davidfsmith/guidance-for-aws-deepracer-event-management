# DeepRacer Events - Role Guide

## Mechanic

### TENETS

* Two’s better than one. One’s better than none.

### REQUIREMENTS

* Be familiar with the car mechanical functions and any issues that may prevent it from racing
* Be familiar with the car web interface - how to load models, calibrate cars, and troubleshoot issues
	* Familiarity with DREM customisations when enrolling the car
* Be familiar with car calibration process as established in: https://bit.ly/dr-car-calibration
	* Be familiar with car flashing process
	* Calibration standards as established in documentation
* Coordinate with Pit Boss(es) as needed
* Follow process established by Pit Crew Lead for the given event
* Provide feedback on the event and processes to the Pit Crew Lead and/or the post-event survey

### RESPONSIBILITIES

* Arrive early (~15 minutes - on time is late) for your shift as applicable to coordinate with the outgoing shift and check in with the Pit Crew Lead
* Verify Mechanic area has appropriate equipment:
	* Spare DeepRacer Car(s)
	* Battery charging station for both Compute (USB-C) and LiPo batteries
	* Car Calibration Stand
	* Display / Keyboard / Mouse / Prepared Flash Drives - for flashing cars if necessary
		* Optional: KVM for large events to help with throughput on flashing cars
	* Label things as needed - status of cars
* Keep Mechanic area neat and clear of bags / drinks / food throughout the event
	* Drinks / food in designated storage area
	* Keep all of this away from the charging area - __NO FOOD OR DRINKS__ in charging area
	* Keep an eye on charging batteries and remove them from chargers once they finish charging - __DO NOT__ leave them on the charge once full
	* Be aware that the batteries discharge overnight (car batteries if the USB-C cable is left plugged in) - check them in the morning before the start of the event
* Verify with Pit Boss(es) that track-side table has the appropriate equipment - extra batteries and minimize number of “spare” cars
* If Mechanic area is being shared - coordinate with other users (video streaming) as necessary
* Maintain at least three (3) cars “ready to race” out on track with Pit Bosses (or other number specified by Pit Crew Lead)
	* These should be powered on, batteries charged and connected, and calibrated
	* Bring cars on-line and calibrate / prepare as necessary to maintain this minimum number
	* If you are on the first shift of the day/event - cars should be tested on the track prior to being considered “ready to race”
	* 3 cars includes what is out at the Pit Boss table - try to minimize the number of powered on cars where possible
* __Verify all cars prior to delivering to the track__ - manual operation and loading a sample model to test inference - that the wheels move in the correct direction and the car steers as applicable
	* The car does not need to be placed on the track, just hold the car in mid-air with the RC motor turned on and a model loaded, press “start” and the wheels should spin and the steering will jiggle if everything is working correctly
	* When cars are powered on - recalibrate prior to delivering to the track
* Receive batteries from Pit Boss(es) and charge as necessary - deliver fresh batteries to track-side table as necessary
* In situations where network conditions dictate - the Pit Crew Lead may request that the Mechanic minimize the number of devices on the network - if this is the case, coordinate with the Pit Boss(es) to minimize the number of powered on “spare” cars.
* Work with other Pit Crew to proactively resolve any issues as they arise - prioritizing those that prevent racing - and assist in escalation to the Pit Crew Lead as necessary.
* At the end of your shift, coordinate with the incoming shift and check out with the Pit Crew Lead before you leave
	* Coordinate information on flashed cars, calibrated cars, staging cars, label broken cars at end of event
* Large Events:
	* If there are multiple mechanics - one should be out at the track as a minimum and the secondary / tertiary can be working on cars in the mechanic area if necessary
