# DeepRacer Events - Role Guide

## Timekeeper

Timekeepers are responsible for accurately recording laps and serving as a secondary observer to the Track Boss,
focused on laps and lap times.

### TENETS

* Accuracy matters

### REQUIREMENTS

* Wear appropriate clothing (Depending on the type of event):
	* DeepRacer Pit Crew Shirt or DeepRacer branded t-shirt
* Be familiar with any rules for the event - including number of resets and order of racers
* Review Event Info Sheet
* Be familiar with the timekeeping interface in DREM
	* Familiar with components of timing solution to help with troubleshooting (do not need deep expertise, just
roughly how it works to point out problems)
	* Familiar with switching from automatic to manual timing and back if necessary
	* Retrieve all necessary login credentials for timing solution
* Coordinate with Track Boss / Pit Boss / Racers as needed
* Follow process established by Pit Crew Lead for the given event
* Provide feedback on the event and processes to the Pit Crew Lead and/or the post-event survey

### RESPONSIBILITIES

* Arrive early (~15 minutes - on time is late) for your shift as applicable to coordinate with the outgoing shift and check in with the Pit Crew Lead
* Select the correct racer by confirming with the Pit Boss / Track Boss / Racer as applicable
* Manual Timing
	* Start the timekeeping interface/stopwatch when the track boss signals - upon completing a lap (when the car breaks the plane of the start/finish line) record the time by pressing the lap button on the timing interface or stopwatch.
* Automatic Timing
	* Ensure you are connected to the timing device directly in the browser
		* If one lap is a clear outlier from other laps - verify with the Track Boss and disqualify as appropriate
	* Call out “1 minute”, “30 seconds” and “Final Lap” as appropriate
	* When the timer reaches zero, the racer is allowed to complete the lap
	* Log Car Resets as established for the event and using the appropriate method
	* If there is an issue with the car on track - pause the clock pending notification from the Track Boss
	* Confirm with the Track Boss on validity of a specific lap(s)
	* Work with other Pit Crew to proactively resolve any issues as they arise - prioritising those that prevent racing - and assist in escalation to the Pit Crew Lead as necessary.
* At the end of your shift, coordinate with the incoming shift and check out with the Pit Crew Lead before you leave

Large Events:

* Verify rules and racer order (if established) prior to your shift to expedite racing
* Rotation with Track Boss - when multiple people are assigned to Track Boss, have the spares serve as Timekeeper - Example: Track Boss, take a break, then when you are next up, serve as the time keeper, then Track Boss again.
